Response To SONA 2023

Nkumpi Trading welcomes the State of the Nation Address (SONA) 2023, delivered by the President of the republic Cyril Ramaphosa.

Published by NkumpiTrading, 01 Mar 2023

Nkumpi Trading welcomes the State of the Nation Address (SONA) 2023, delivered by the President of the republic Cyril Ramaphosa on February 09, 2023. However, the government could have gone further in addressing the challenges facing the municipal planning debacle and South Africa’s industrial policy.

However, the SONA 2023 reflected an uncertain analysis of the environment in which we co-exist. Further, it demonstrated and amplified the country's ordinary difficulties, particularly in the energy sector. Significantly, we acknowledge the support given to Eskom by recognising the public entity's pivotal economic and social role.

The pronouncement of renewable energy investments was central to the SONA 2023 to fulfil the objectives of the Integrated Resource Plan (IRP) energy action plan and those tabled in the electricity regulation amendment bill. Much of these investment projects are concentrated in the Eastern Cape, Northern Cape, and Western Cape, as mentioned at the Green Hydrogen Sumit 2022.

These energy projects will create considerable employment and an influx of inward migration to these areas of opportunity. Therefore, the failure of the president to emphasize and zero in on the importance of municipal planning amidst these land-use changes is dire, considering the appalling local municipal performance. The president missed an opportunity to bring probable confidence to the planning fraternity.

In addition, the nineteen (19) renewable energy projects will not only have positive spill-offs in energy generation, research and development (R&D), human settlement and employment creation. Still, they will also reignite the stagnant industrial policy. The president’s failure to acknowledge the industrial prospects coupled with the renewable energy projects is concerning. Over the years, the South African government has seen alarming unemployment rates reach record highs of 32,5% in 2023 (Quarterly labour Survey.2023). This is indicative of the need to formulate strategic plans for employment creation.

As scholars of economic planning, we believe that Africa, as late industrializers, needs to revise their industrial policy and invest significantly in industries. Moreover, by industrialising townships and rural areas through decentralization processes, there will be a significant economic shift towards the poorest strata of our economy. This will increase informal businesses formalizing through various incentive models tailor-made by the national treasury. Therefore, industrialisation will significantly create sustainable jobs and have considerable land-use change, promoting spatial transformation and balancing, consequently shrinking the socio-economic inequality and urban-rural divide in South Africa.
