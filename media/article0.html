Exponential increase of fossil fuel emissions

Future cities must be built on the bedrock of green economies and renewable energy.

South Africa has been plagued by energy challenges and the burden has increased over the past half a decade.
Being a coal-based economy,South Africa has a responsibility to significantly reduce their carbon footprint
to fulfil the sustainability development goals.

South African cities are today vulnerable to disinvestments, unreliable and inefficient due to their overconsumption of non-renewable energy;

  • Lack of infrastructure maintenance
  • Uncertain energy policies
  • lacklustre leadership

which consequently has resulted in continuous blackouts and/or loadshedding.

In order to curb the exponential increase of fossil fuel emissions, climate anthropogenic factors need to be reduced,
through lowering the energy costs and improving the reliability through using green-infrastructure and renewable energy.
Such initiatives would see an increase in renewable energy providers investing in wind farms; hydroelectric plants;
and solar energy farms.
